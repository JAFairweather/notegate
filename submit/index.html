Before you type anything, understand what this does and does not hide. Your message and any follow-up dialogue are end-to-end encrypted; no server holds them, and the message you send carries no signature that identifies you. But the relays that carry it can see your IP address and when you sent it, and if you attach files, the blob hosts that store them see the same — plus the rough size, never the content or name. If your situation is serious, open this page in Tor Browser and do not use a work or home network. Losing the recovery phrase shown after sending permanently closes the reply channel — that is by design.
Files are encrypted on this device before upload — hosts store unnamed ciphertext, padded so even the exact size is hidden. 100 MB per file.
